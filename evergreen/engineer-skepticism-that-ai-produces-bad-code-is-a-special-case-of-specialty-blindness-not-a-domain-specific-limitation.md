# Engineer skepticism that AI produces bad code is a special case of specialty-blindness not a domain-specific limitation

Source: [[thought-session-what-the-g-in-agi-actually-means|Thought Session: What the G in AGI Actually Means]]

The standard engineering complaint about language models — that they produce bad code, that they are unreliable, that the prompts are "just text" — appears to be a domain-specific assessment based on engineers' rigorous standards for the artifact AI produces in their specialty. It is not. It is a special case of the more general specialty-blindness: an evaluator using the criteria of their own field to judge a tool whose value lives in spanning every field, including dozens that they have not tested it on.

The structural similarity to other specialty-bound assessments is exact. The biochemist concludes AI cannot replace their experimental design work. The financial analyst concludes AI cannot do their valuations end-to-end. The author concludes AI cannot write a book worth reading. The engineer concludes AI cannot ship production code. Each is correct in narrow terms. Each misses that the same tool does that 60-to-90-percent job in every other specialty too, and that the cross-domain reach is the actual novelty.

The reason the engineering case feels different is that engineers are closer to the tool — they understand its surface behavior better than other specialists — so their dismissal carries more apparent authority. But the structure of the misjudgment is identical, and recognizing this is rhetorically useful: the people best-positioned to perceive AI's mechanism are also subject to the same blindness as everyone else, and for the same reason.

**Open questions:**
- Does explicit awareness of this structural pattern help engineers update their assessment, or do specialty-bound criteria remain dominant even when their structure is named?
- The same structural argument should apply to AI researchers themselves — who are deepest in the specialty of building these tools. Does that community show similar blindness in different form?
