# AI agents lack consequential agency because they have no homeostatic stakes in their own decisions

Source: [[thought-session-what-the-g-in-agi-actually-means|Thought Session: What the G in AGI Actually Means]]

The framework developed in *The Engine Below* identifies stakes — specifically, the maintenance of a homeostatic boundary against entropy — as constitutive of agency in living systems. Valence translates prediction error into the urge to correct it; without stakes, prediction error is just signal, and signal without valence has no motor to drive action.

Current AI architectures, including large language models and agent systems built on them, lack any equivalent of this. A language model has no boundary that matters in the relevant sense. It does not eat. It does not flinch. When its predictions fail, no metabolic crisis follows. The model performs computation that simulates the surface features of decision-making, but nothing in the system is closer to dissolution when the decision is wrong. Consequence-blind computation is not agency; it is computation directed by something that does have agency.

This has direct implications for the design of agentic AI. An agent system cannot be expected to develop genuine self-direction by improvements to model capability alone. The capability is structural; the agency requires stakes. Whatever appears as "autonomous behavior" in current agents is more accurately described as the operationalization of human-supplied direction across a longer horizon than chat allows.

**Open questions:**
- Could engineered stakes — a budget, revocable compute access, resource constraints tied to performance — constitute homeostasis-equivalent stakes for a synthetic system? This question is parked in *The Engine Below*'s "Gaps Identified" and remains unresolved. Its resolution determines whether this claim is structural or merely current.
- Is "consequential agency" a binary property or a graded one? If graded, where on the scale do current AI systems sit, and where would they need to be to count as having genuine agency?
