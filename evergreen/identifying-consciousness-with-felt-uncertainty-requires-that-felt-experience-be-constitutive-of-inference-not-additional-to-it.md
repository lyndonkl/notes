# Identifying consciousness with felt uncertainty requires that felt experience be constitutive of inference, not additional to it

Source: [[a-world-appears-a-journey-into-consciousness-thread|A World Appears: A Journey into Consciousness]]

Mark Solms's claim that "consciousness is felt uncertainty" — and Karl Friston's parallel claim that consciousness is "nothing grander than inference about my future" — is a specific philosophical move. It identifies the felt dimension of conscious experience with the system-internal character of uncertainty-minimizing inference. On this view, what it is like to feel hungry just is what the prediction-error signal of unmet homeostasis is from inside the system. There is no separate "felt urgency" added to the mechanism; the mechanism's internal character is the felt urgency.

This is a specific resolution of the hard problem of consciousness — the question of why mechanistic accounts of cognition do not, by themselves, predict the felt dimension. The Solms/Friston resolution dissolves the question by denying that there is anything additional to predict. The hard problem appears hard, on this view, because we are asking the wrong question — assuming that the mechanism and the felt experience are two separable things when in fact the felt experience just is what the mechanism is from a particular vantage point.

The price tag is steep. To accept this resolution, you have to accept that any system doing inference of the right kind has, to some degree, a felt dimension. A virus minimizing surprise has some primitive felt-something. A thermostat minimizing surprise relative to a setpoint either has a felt-something, or there is some structural feature of the inference (complexity, recursion, social modeling) that turns the lights on partway up the staircase. Either you commit to graded panpsychism, or you commit to a threshold criterion within the inference framework.

This is the resolution Pollan introduces through Friston and Solms. It is one of several positions on offer, and accepting it requires accepting its commitments — there is no free lunch where the hard problem dissolves without a corresponding metaphysical bill.

**Open questions:**
- If the felt dimension is constitutive of inference, where on the staircase does it begin? Is it graded all the way down, or is there a threshold?
- If consciousness is felt uncertainty, what predicts that some kinds of uncertainty-reduction are accompanied by felt experience while others (presumably thermostats, simple sensors) are not?
