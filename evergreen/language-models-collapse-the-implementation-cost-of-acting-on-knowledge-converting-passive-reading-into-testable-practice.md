# Language models collapse the implementation cost of acting on knowledge converting passive reading into testable practice

Source: [[thought-session-what-the-g-in-agi-actually-means|Thought Session: What the G in AGI Actually Means]]

Knowledge acquired through reading has historically existed in a different category from knowledge acquired through practice. Reading a corporate finance textbook gives you a model of how valuation works; acting on that knowledge — actually valuing companies, defending the valuations, observing the outcomes — required either becoming a finance analyst or hiring one. The implementation cost of converting reading into action was, for most non-specialist readers, prohibitive. The reading remained potential.

Language models change this. The reader who absorbed the textbook can now encode its frameworks as skills, run them on real data drawn from their own life, and empirically test whether the frameworks hold. The implementation cost is not zero, but it is small enough that the reading can move from potential to operational. A book that previously sat as an interesting framework can become a tested instrument.

This connects to a broader epistemological claim: that testing a belief against reality requires operational criteria, not intuitive plausibility. AI is the tool that lets non-specialists construct operational criteria for beliefs they previously could only intuit. It is, in a strong sense, the operationalization-of-belief-testing machine. The implication is that years of curiosity-driven reading that previously could not be acted on are now acquirable as actual capability — provided the reader has the systems knowledge to do the encoding.

**Open questions:**
- For which kinds of knowledge does this collapse work cleanly, and for which does it not? Knowledge that requires physical embodiment (surgery, athletic technique, lab craft) seems harder to operationalize this way than knowledge that lives in text and reasoning.
- Does the operationalization preserve the depth of the original framework, or does it flatten it? A skill encoding Damodaran's valuation may capture the procedure but lose the judgment behind the procedure.
