# Agency in AI agents is loaned by the human and is not intrinsic to the agent

Source: [[thought-session-what-the-g-in-agi-actually-means|Thought Session: What the G in AGI Actually Means]]

Because AI agents lack the homeostatic stakes that, on the framework developed in *The Engine Below*, are constitutive of intrinsic agency, an agent's capacity to act in the world is structurally borrowed. The agent acts, but the *reason for acting* — the purpose that organizes the action toward something rather than nothing — comes from a human directing the system. The direction is what gives the agent its agency.

This makes agency the third item in a structural pattern alongside knowledge and interface. Each describes what the LLM as a tool brings on its own and what the human supplies to make it useful: broad domain knowledge is intrinsic, but specific purpose is loaned through context engineering; the universal text interface is intrinsic, but the shaping work of using it is loaned; capability for action is intrinsic, but the agency that directs the action toward ends is loaned through human intention. The substrate is general; the realization is loaned.

The practical consequence is that "autonomous AI agents" is a misleading description of what current systems are. They are not autonomous in the strict sense; they are humans acting at greater leverage through tools that handle the cognitive heavy lifting. The agent's apparent autonomy is the human's intention extended across more context, more time, and more steps than direct execution would allow.

**Open questions:**
- If engineered stakes can constitute homeostasis-equivalent agency, the loaned/intrinsic distinction collapses — and the conceptual move that separates "directed AI" from "autonomous AI" becomes a matter of degree rather than kind. The honest framing is that this collapse is possible.
- The structural symmetry with knowledge and interface (intrinsic substrate, loaned realization) suggests this is a more general pattern of how the LLM functions as a tool. Are there other axes where the same intrinsic/loaned split appears?
