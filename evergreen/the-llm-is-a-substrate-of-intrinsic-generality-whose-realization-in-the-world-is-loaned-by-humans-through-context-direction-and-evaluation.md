# The LLM is a substrate of intrinsic generality whose realization in the world is loaned by humans through context, direction, and evaluation

Source: [[thought-session-what-the-g-in-agi-actually-means|Thought Session: What the G in AGI Actually Means]]

The argument that has emerged across this thought session has a single organizing pattern. Everything the LLM is — its broad domain knowledge, its universal text interface, its capacity for action, its average competence across every field — comes from the model itself, before any user shows up. Everything that turns this substrate into useful work — the specific purpose of any given task, the context engineering that scopes the model toward that purpose, the agency that directs action toward ends, the evaluation that closes the loop — comes from the human operating the tool. The substrate is intrinsic; the realization is loaned.

This pattern is not a metaphor; it is the structural relationship that ties together a dozen specific claims. Knowledge is intrinsic; context engineering is loaned. The universal text interface is intrinsic; the shaping work of using it is loaned. Capability for action is intrinsic; the agency that gives the action purpose is loaned. The model's average output is intrinsic; the evaluation of whether the output was the right output is loaned. Even the generality that makes the LLM a historically distinctive tool is intrinsic; the realization of that generality for any specific purpose is loaned, and the amount loaned varies by the operator's depth/breadth profile and systems knowledge.

If this is the spine of how to understand the LLM as a tool, several practical consequences follow. The "we have surpassed AGI" feeling lives at the high-loan end, where compositional builders are realizing the intrinsic generality across many domains. The "AGI doesn't exist" complaint lives at the low-loan end, where specialists treating the model as a domain oracle are getting the average behavior the substrate produces without their shaping. The design implication is that improvement of agentic AI is improvement of the loop — the architecture in which intrinsic substrate and loaned realization meet.

**Open questions:**
- Is the intrinsic/loaned distinction permanent, or does it weaken as model capability increases? At minimum, the loaned half could shrink (better defaults reducing how much shaping is needed) without the intrinsic half ever absorbing the loaned half entirely.
- The distinction depends on the agency claim being correct. If engineered stakes turn out to constitute homeostasis-equivalent agency in some future architecture, the agency-loaned half collapses, and the spine rebalances toward more being intrinsic.
