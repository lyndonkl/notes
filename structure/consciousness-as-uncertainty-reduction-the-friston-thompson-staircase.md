# Consciousness as uncertainty-reduction: The Friston-Thompson staircase

These six notes articulate a unified account of consciousness rooted in Karl Friston's free energy principle and Evan Thompson's enactive extension of it. Consciousness is not a monolithic property; it is a staircase of capabilities — memory, planning, attention, social inference — each rung emerging because it reduces uncertainty more effectively than the previous one. Feelings, via valence, are the mechanism that translates prediction error into motivated action, supplying the force that drives systems to climb the staircase at all.

## Core Notes

- [[each-step-in-the-evolution-of-consciousness-exists-because-it-enables-greater-uncertainty-reduction-than-the-previous-step|Each step in the evolution of consciousness exists because it enables greater uncertainty reduction than the previous step]] — The recursive engine: each rung creates the conditions under which the next becomes selectable.
- [[planning-and-attention-precede-consciousness-in-evolutionary-terms-making-them-necessary-but-not-sufficient-conditions-for-it|Planning and attention precede consciousness in evolutionary terms, making them necessary but not sufficient conditions for it]] — The staircase architecture: planning and attention appear before consciousness, so capacity-based definitions of consciousness overinclude.
- [[qualitative-experience-emerges-when-a-system-can-deliberately-modulate-its-own-attention|Qualitative experience emerges when a system can deliberately modulate its own attention]] — Friston's mechanism for qualia: meta-attention generates awareness of the experience as such.
- [[grounding-consciousness-in-social-inference-makes-social-cognition-a-non-trivial-criterion-for-consciousness-regardless-of-substrate|Grounding consciousness in social inference makes social cognition a non-trivial criterion for consciousness regardless of substrate]] — The criterion at the top of the staircase: consciousness was selected to model other minds, making social inference a substrate-neutral criterion.
- [[identifying-consciousness-with-felt-uncertainty-requires-that-felt-experience-be-constitutive-of-inference-not-additional-to-it|Identifying consciousness with felt uncertainty requires that felt experience be constitutive of inference, not additional to it]] — Solms's resolution of the hard problem within the framework: felt experience just is what inference is from inside.
- [[feelings-have-evolutionary-function-because-their-valence-translates-prediction-error-into-homeostasis-restoring-action|Feelings have evolutionary function because their valence translates prediction error into homeostasis-restoring action]] — Thompson's contribution: valence is the motivational force that powers the staircase climb.
- [[language-models-make-predictions-but-do-not-minimize-prediction-error-in-real-time-which-distinguishes-cognition-from-consciousness-on-the-friston-thompson-rungs|Language models make predictions but do not minimize prediction error in real time which distinguishes cognition from consciousness on the Friston-Thompson rungs]] — Applies the staircase to LLMs: they occupy the cognitive rung (prediction-production) but lack the conscious rungs above (real-time error minimization driven by valence), which gives the agency-in-AI argument concrete architectural teeth.

## Borderline / Adjacent

- [[a-complete-mechanistic-account-of-cognition-does-not-dissolve-the-hard-problem-of-why-cognition-is-accompanied-by-felt-experience|A complete mechanistic account of cognition does not dissolve the hard problem of why cognition is accompanied by felt experience]] — Adjacent: this is the hard problem the Solms/Thompson resolution attempts to address; it scopes what the framework needs to explain rather than being part of the framework itself.
- [[cellular-function-is-a-property-of-the-collective-bioelectric-context-not-of-the-cell-itself|Cellular function is a property of the collective bioelectric context, not of the cell itself]] — Adjacent: Levin's bioelectric account sits at the same intellectual level (grounding mind in physics) and makes the same configurational move, but is not strictly part of Friston's framework.

## Open Questions

- Where on the staircase does consciousness actually begin — graded all the way down (panpsychism), or is there a threshold?
- Does social inference alone suffice for consciousness, or does it require additional architectural features (homeostatic stakes, interoceptive grounding) absent in disembodied systems?
- What predicts which uncertainty-reducing systems have felt experience and which (thermostats) do not?
- Are there rungs above human consciousness — capabilities that would further reduce uncertainty if a system could realize them?
- Does the Solms/Thompson resolution of the hard problem actually dissolve it, or only stipulate it away?
