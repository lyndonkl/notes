# Loaned Agency: Why the Human-Agent Loop Is Permanent, Not Transitional

AI agents lack consequential agency because they lack homeostatic stakes — the architectural ingredient that, on the Friston-Thompson framework, gives living systems goals to pursue and valence-driven correction in real time. Current language models occupy the cognitive rung (they make predictions) but not the conscious rungs (they do not minimize prediction error in real time), which is why engineered stakes — budgets, revocable compute, performance-tied constraints — cannot constitute homeostasis-equivalent agency. The agency that directs agent action toward ends is structurally loaned by the human operator, which makes the human-agent loop a permanent architectural feature rather than scaffolding to be removed, identifies human subjective satisfaction as the terminal feedback signal, and predicts that a closed-loop AI economy is structurally impossible while loaned-agency holds.

## Open Questions

- What would credible architectures for real-time prediction-error minimization in synthetic systems even look like? The question is technical, not just philosophical, and the field has not converged on candidate designs.
- Is "consequential agency" binary or graded? If graded, where on the scale do current AI systems sit, and how would partial agency interact with loaned agency from a human operator?
- Human consequential agency is itself partial (akrasia, addiction, sunk-cost). If humans supply the agency the loop needs, the partiality of human agency complicates the "human satisfaction as feedback" claim. (Held for future session.)
