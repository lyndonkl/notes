# Why statistical priors trump detailed analysis in prediction

These notes orbit a single paradox of forecasting: the more detailed a mental model feels, the more confident and the less accurate it becomes. Specificity masquerades as accuracy; the conjunction fallacy makes richly imagined plans underestimate variability; the inside view feels more credible than the outside view precisely because it is richer, not because it is more reliable. Yet Bayesian reasoning — the formal escape route — fails in practice when people can't estimate the inputs it assumes are given, which is why relative-odds reasoning provides the actual cognitive interface for belief updates.

## Core Notes

- [[reference-class-forecasting-outperforms-inside-view-estimation-for-project-timelines|Reference class forecasting outperforms inside-view estimation for project timelines]] — the central empirical claim: statistical base rates from comparable past projects beat detailed case-specific estimation
- [[the-inside-view-feels-more-credible-than-the-outside-view-because-it-contains-specific-details-not-because-it-is-more-reliable|The inside view feels more credible than the outside view because it contains specific details, not because it is more reliable]] — the cognitive mechanism: specificity is persuasive independent of calibration
- [[detail-in-a-plan-increases-perceived-accuracy-but-decreases-actual-accuracy|Detail in a plan increases perceived accuracy but decreases actual accuracy]] — the conjunction-fallacy consequence: adding detail narrows the predicted path and inflates its probability beyond what the joint event warrants
- [[bayesian-reasoning-in-practice-requires-estimating-inputs-that-the-theory-assumes-are-given|Bayesian reasoning in practice requires estimating inputs that the theory assumes are given]] — the practical obstacle: the formal corrective to these biases is unusable if its inputs (priors, likelihoods) are themselves guesses
- [[reasoning-in-relative-odds-is-more-intuitive-than-reasoning-in-probabilities-when-updating-beliefs-with-new-evidence|Reasoning in relative odds is more intuitive than reasoning in probabilities when updating beliefs with new evidence]] — the usable workaround: odds-ratio form reduces Bayesian updating to a multiplication humans can actually perform

## Open Questions

- When does the inside view actually outperform the outside view? Presumably the reference class has to be appropriate — what defines appropriateness?
- Is the relative-odds form a genuine solution to the input-estimation problem, or does it just shift the guessing from probabilities to ratios?
- Planning fallacy research shows that even forecasters who know about the inside-view bias still fall prey to it. What intervention actually works in practice?
