# Intelligence as a property of social systems, not individual agents

Intelligence scales through social organization and institutional structure, not through individual cognitive upgrades. This reframes both AI alignment (from training individuals to designing institutions) and multi-agent system architecture (from optimizing single agents to designing the coordination layer). The cluster draws from multiple independent sources — a theoretical paper on agentic AI, an engineering investigation of OTP, and a thought session on what "general" in AGI actually means — that converged on the same organizational science frame, now extended by the observation that the LLM itself instantiates the principle: its intrinsic knowledge is distilled collective human cognition, and its realization in the world is loaned by the broader social context.

- [[organizational-science-is-an-untapped-design-source-for-multi-agent-ai-systems|Organizational science is an untapped design source for multi-agent AI systems]] — Applies the insight: a century of organizational science research has already solved many coordination problems that multi-agent AI is trying to reinvent from scratch
- [[scalable-ai-alignment-requires-institutional-templates-not-individual-virtue|Scalable AI alignment requires institutional templates not individual virtue]] — Reframes alignment: RLHF optimizes individual agents, but scalable alignment requires persistent institutional structures (roles, norms, protocols) that govern agent behavior
- [[otp-models-mechanical-orchestration-but-multi-agent-ai-needs-organic-orchestration|OTP models mechanical orchestration but multi-agent AI needs organic orchestration]] — Tests the frame against existing systems: OTP is organizational science but only the deterministic, mechanical subset; human organizations operate through fuzzy guidelines, not binary supervision specs

- [[llms-are-the-first-tools-whose-general-purpose-knowledge-is-contained-in-the-tool-itself-rather-than-supplied-by-the-user|LLMs are the first tools whose general-purpose knowledge is contained in the tool itself rather than supplied by the user]] — Makes the social-intelligence claim concrete at the tool level: the model's knowledge is the residue of its training corpus — collective social production crystallized into a substrate

## Open Questions

- What do institutional templates for AI agents actually look like in practice — what's the format, the enforcement mechanism, the update cycle?
- How do soft institutional templates get enforced without human-social mechanisms (cultural pressure, hiring/firing, performance reviews)?
- Is there a continuum between mechanical orchestration (OTP) and organic orchestration (human organizations), and where on that continuum should multi-agent AI systems aim?
- What organizational science research is most directly transferable to multi-agent AI design?
